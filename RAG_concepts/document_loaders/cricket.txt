Cricket is one of the world’s most popular sports, played professionally and recreationally in more than 100 countries and followed by an estimated 2.5 billion fans worldwide. It is contested between two teams of 11 players on a circular or oval field with a 22-yard (20.12-meter) pitch at its center. The batting side attempts to score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets or by striking boundaries worth 4 runs or 6 runs, while the bowling and fielding side tries to dismiss batters through methods such as bowled, caught, run out, or leg before wicket (LBW).

International cricket is played in three primary formats: Test matches, lasting up to five days with unlimited overs; One Day Internationals (ODIs), where each team faces 50 overs; and Twenty20 (T20) matches, limited to 20 overs per side. An over consists of six legal deliveries. The sport originated in England and is governed globally by the International Cricket Council (ICC). Prestigious tournaments such as the ICC Cricket World Cup and ICC T20 World Cup attract hundreds of millions of viewers. Success in cricket demands a combination of technical skill, strategy, endurance, teamwork, and mental resilience, making it one of the most complex and captivating team sports in the world.
